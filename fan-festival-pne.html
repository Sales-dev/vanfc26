Transit & Parking

Getting to the PNE

TransLink is by far the easiest option — parking at the PNE fills fast on busy days. Full transit guide →

🚇
SkyTrain Expo Line → Renfrew Station
Take the Expo Line toward King George or Production Way. Exit at Renfrew Station. From there it's a 10–15 min walk east along Hastings St, or catch Bus 14 or Bus 16 eastbound for 2 stops.
🚌
Bus 14 / Bus 16 along Hastings Street
Both routes run directly along E Hastings St — hop on at any stop downtown and ride to the Renfrew / Kaslo stop right at the PNE entrance. Frequent service during events.
🚲
Bike Share (Mobi)
Mobi docking stations near the PNE main entrance. A scenic option via the Central Valley Greenway or Adanac bikeway from downtown (~30 min). mobibikes.ca →
🚗
Driving & Parking
Paid lots on the PNE grounds. Expect lots to fill early on BC Place match days — thousands of fans will be at both venues. Street parking limited on E Hastings. Transit is strongly recommended on those days.

What to Expect

What's at the Fan Festival

The Fan Festival is more than just concerts. The full grounds are open daily from when doors open through to the end of the night's concert.

On the Grounds

  • ⚽ Match screenings — giant screens, all group stage and knockout games
  • 🎸 Free Park Stage — daily live music beyond the amphitheatre headliners
  • 🍜 Food village — diverse vendors and international street food
  • 🏪 FIFA merchandise — official World Cup gear and souvenirs
  • 🎮 Interactive experiences — FIFA gaming, skills challenges, photo moments
  • 🌍 Cultural zones — celebrating the 48 nations in the tournament

Good to Know

  • ✅ All-ages event — great for families
  • ✅ Free entry to the grounds every day
  • ✅ No ticket needed to watch match screenings
  • 📵 Security check at the gate — arrive early on busy days
  • 🎒 No large bags or glass containers permitted
  • 🌤 Vancouver in June/July — warm days, cool evenings, bring a layer

FAQ

Common Questions

Is the Fan Festival really free?
Yes — entry to the Fan Festival grounds is completely free every day. You can walk in, watch match screenings on the giant screens, enjoy the Park Stage, food, and interactive experiences without paying anything. The only paid option is reserved seating in the Amphitheatre for headline concerts. A limited number of GA lawn spots in the Amphitheatre are also free but first-come, first-served.
Do I need a ticket to watch the games on the big screens?
No ticket required for match screenings. The giant screens throughout the grounds are free to watch. Just show up, find a good spot, and enjoy the game. Arrive earlier on Canada match days — crowds are expected to be enormous.
How do I get reserved concert tickets?
Tickets for reserved amphitheatre seating are available through TicketLeader.ca. Public tickets went on sale April 2, 2026. Some shows are already selling fast — check TicketLeader for availability and pricing for individual nights.
Can I still get the free GA lawn spots for concerts?
Yes, a limited number of free General Admission lawn spots are available at the Amphitheatre each night. These are first-come, first-served — no ticket needed, but the gates will close once capacity is reached. For big shows (Metric, Arkells, Kaytranada), plan to arrive 60–90 minutes early. For quieter mid-week nights, 30 minutes early is usually enough.
What's the best transit option?
The Expo Line SkyTrain to Renfrew Station is the fastest option — then either walk 10–15 min east along Hastings or catch Bus 14/16 for 2 stops to the PNE entrance. On match days at BC Place, thousands of people are on transit — give yourself extra time. Avoid driving if you can; parking fills fast.
Is this the same as the PNE Summer Fair?
No — these are separate events. The FIFA Fan Festival runs June 11 – July 19, 2026 and is focused on the World Cup. The PNE Summer Fair (rides, exhibits, food) runs its usual late August dates (roughly Aug 22 – Sep 1). The Fan Festival uses the PNE's amphitheatre and grounds during the tournament window.
Is it family-friendly?
Yes — the Fan Festival is an all-ages event. Families with kids are very welcome. The grounds have a wide range of activities beyond just concerts. Evening concerts may run late, so plan accordingly if coming with young children.
