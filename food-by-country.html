Food Guide

Where to Find Your Country's Food in Vancouver

Vancouver is one of the world's most multicultural food cities. Whatever nation you're supporting, there's an excellent chance you can find authentic food from home. Here's where to look.

The Americas

Brazil

The Brazilian community in Vancouver centres around the West End and Kitsilano. Boteco (Commercial Drive area) serves coxinha and pastéis. Several churrasqueira-style restaurants operate in the suburbs. Search for Brazilian community Facebook groups — they maintain the most current restaurant listings.

Mexico

Excellent Mexican food is citywide. La Taqueria (Hastings, Main Street locations) does outstanding tacos. Sal y Limon (Robson Street) is popular for street-food style Mexican in the West End. East Vancouver has several family-run taquerias preferred by local Mexicans over tourist-facing spots.

Argentina & Uruguay

Empanadas at La Casita (Commercial Drive). Argentina-influenced steakhouses exist in Gastown and Yaletown — look for churrasco and chimichurri on menus.

Colombia

Vancouver has a growing Colombian community. Arepa spots and Colombian bakeries can be found in East Vancouver. Patacon Pisao near Commercial Drive serves Colombian street food.

USA

American BBQ, Southern fried chicken, and New York-style deli food are well represented. Memphis Blues BBQ (multiple locations) does outstanding slow-smoked ribs. The Gastown area has excellent American diner food.

Europe

England / UK & Ireland

Multiple genuine British and Irish pubs: The Irish Heather (Gastown), The Blarney Stone (Gastown), Doolin's Irish Pub (downtown). Fish and chips at Go Fish near Granville Island and at Steveston village (Richmond).

France

French bakeries and bistros are distributed across the city. Beaucoup Bakery (Fir Street) is outstanding for croissants and viennoiserie. Thierry (Alberni Street) is a high-end French patisserie. French bistro food at Pastis (West Side).

Germany

Schnitzel's in various forms around the city. German delicatessens in the suburbs (North Van, Burnaby) stock imported German products. Oktoberfest-style events run annually — the World Cup timing means extended German beer garden pop-ups are likely.

Italy

Vancouver has an excellent Italian tradition. Commercial Drive has the original Italian neighbourhood — Caffe Calabria and Café Napoli for espresso culture. Downtown: Ask for Luigi for fresh pasta. Italian delis throughout East Vancouver stock imported products.

Portugal

Portuguese custard tarts (pastéis de nata) at Nata's Pastries (multiple locations). Portuguese chicken at several piri-piri spots. The Portuguese community is concentrated in the South Granville and Kingsway areas.

Spain

Spanish tapas bars are popular across Vancouver. Cobre (Gastown) does contemporary Spanish. Bodega on Main has a genuine tapas menu with a good Rioja list.

Asia & Middle East

Japan

Vancouver arguably has the best Japanese food in North America outside major US cities. Miku (Burrard Landing) for flame-seared aburi sushi. Ajisai (multiple locations) for accessible sushi. Ramen: Hokkaido Ramen Santouka (Robson) and Ramen Danbo (multiple). Richmond has an even greater concentration of Japanese restaurants.

South Korea

Korean food is exceptional in Vancouver. Muri (Robson) for Korean fried chicken. Hanwoori for Korean BBQ. The Kingsway corridor in Burnaby has the densest concentration of Korean restaurants — accessible by SkyTrain to Metrotown.

Morocco

Marrakech (West Broadway) serves traditional Moroccan tagine and couscous. North African cuisine appears across several downtown Mediterranean spots.

Iran

North Vancouver has one of Canada's largest Iranian communities. Lonsdale Avenue (accessible by SeaBus) has excellent Persian restaurants for kebab, stew, and saffron rice.

Australia

Australian food culture (flat whites, avocado toast, and quality café culture) has significantly influenced Vancouver's café scene. The "Australian café" model — excellent coffee, brunch food, minimalist design — is everywhere. Kafka's, Prototype Coffee, and dozens more.

Africa

Ethiopia & Eritrea

Vancouver has excellent East African food. Nyala African Cuisine (Commercial Drive) is one of the city's longest-standing Ethiopian restaurants — injera, tibs, and wats in a warm atmosphere. Commercial Drive has a small cluster of East African spots.

Nigeria / West Africa

West African restaurants are fewer but growing. Check Yelp and Google for current listings — this community is well-established in Surrey and Burnaby with several family-run spots serving jollof rice and egusi soup.

South Africa

Braai culture and biltong. Look for South African imports at specialty food stores; for restaurants, the Boerewors Trail community maintains an online directory of South African food spots in the Vancouver area.

Best Neighbourhoods by Cuisine

Richmond (Golden Village): Chinese (Cantonese, Shanghainese, Taiwanese), Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese. Best dim sum in North America. Accessible by Canada Line SkyTrain.

Commercial Drive: Italian, Ethiopian, Latin American, Middle Eastern. Most multicultural neighbourhood.

Kingsway (Burnaby): Korean, Vietnamese, Cantonese. Accessible by SkyTrain.

North Vancouver (Lonsdale): Iranian/Persian, Greek, Mediterranean. Accessible by SeaBus.

Main Street (Mount Pleasant): Contemporary international, farm-to-table, craft beer and cocktails.

Where to Find Your Country’s Food

Vancouver’s immigrant communities have built strong food cultures across the city. Here’s a practical guide to finding food from key World Cup nations.

Morocco & North Africa

Commercial Drive (the Drive) is the heart of North African food in Vancouver. Try Hafsia for Tunisian tagines, or look for Moroccan pastry shops along the Drive. The area is also a natural fan gathering point for the Moroccan national team’s supporters.

Mexico & Central America

Mexican food is everywhere in Vancouver — quality varies. For the real thing, try La Taqueria on Hastings or Tacofino (Vancouver-founded chain with strong Mexican roots). East Hastings has several family-run taquerias serving late into the night.

Portugal & Spain

South Granville and the West Side have a cluster of Portuguese and Spanish restaurants. Chiado on Alberni is the top Portuguese fine dining option. For casual tapas, explore the stretch around Robson and Thurlow.

Japan & South Korea

Robson Street between Burrard and Bute is often called “Little Japan” — the density of Japanese restaurants is remarkable. For Korean food, head to Robson and Denman or Burnaby’s Metrotown area, which has one of the strongest Korean food scenes in Canada.

Brazil & South America

Brazilian restaurants are scattered across the city rather than clustered. Boteco in Kitsilano is a beloved Brazilian bar and kitchen. For Argentine food, El Camino’s on Main Street captures the South American spirit.
