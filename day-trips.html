Best Day Trips from Vancouver During World Cup 2026

Between matches, the mountains, islands, and sea are all within reach. These day trips run under 3 hours from Vancouver — all of them spectacular, all doable without a car if needed.

Whistler

The most popular day trip from Vancouver. Whistler is a world-famous mountain resort town 120km north of the city along the spectacular Sea-to-Sky Highway. In summer, the mountains are open for hiking, mountain biking, gondola rides, and the incredible PEAK 2 PEAK Gondola connecting the two main peaks — the highest and longest gondola of its kind in the world.

The drive takes about 1.5–2 hours (traffic and road conditions permitting). By bus: Epic Rides and Snowbus offer direct service from Vancouver for around $35–50 return; book ahead in summer. In Whistler Village, walk the pedestrian village, eat well, and if the weather is clear, spend time on the mountains.

Timing note: July is peak season in Whistler. Book any gondola or activity tickets in advance online; walk-up prices are higher and popular activities sell out. Leave before 8am for a smooth drive on the Sea-to-Sky.

Victoria (BC Ferry)

Victoria, BC's provincial capital, is a genuinely different city — more British in character, with stunning flower gardens, excellent whale watching, and a charming historic downtown. Getting there is half the experience: the BC Ferry from Tsawwassen (45 minutes south of Vancouver by transit or car) takes 1h 35min to Swartz Bay, then 30 minutes to downtown Victoria.

Allow a full day minimum — you'll want 3–4 hours in the city after accounting for travel. Highlights: Butchart Gardens (world-famous flower gardens, 20km north of Victoria), the BC Museum on the inner harbour, whale watching tours, and the famous Afternoon Tea at the Empress Hotel.

Book BC Ferry tickets in advance for summer — July is extremely busy. Return ferry prices are around $65–75/person. Alternatively, floatplane flights from Vancouver Harbour to Victoria Harbour take 25 minutes (around $150+ each way).

Squamish & the Sea-to-Sky

Halfway between Vancouver and Whistler, Squamish has become one of BC's best outdoor adventure towns. The Sea-to-Sky Gondola rises 885m above Howe Sound to a summit with a suspension bridge, hiking trails, and panoramic views. Admission is $54 for adults.

Shannon Falls — 335m of vertical drop, the third-highest waterfall in BC — is directly next to the gondola base and free to visit. The Stawamus Chief granite monolith draws rock climbers from around the world; hikers can take the backside trails to summit viewpoints.

Squamish is 65km from Vancouver (about 1 hour) — very doable as a half-day trip. Combine with a Whistler overnight if you want more time in the mountains.

Horseshoe Bay & Howe Sound

Just 25km west of Vancouver, Horseshoe Bay is a charming ferry terminal village at the mouth of Howe Sound. The village itself has good fish and chips, a small marina, and mountain-framed water views.

From Horseshoe Bay, take the 20-minute BC Ferry to Bowen Island — a quiet island community with hiking trails, a small village, and a completely different pace from Vancouver. Perfect for half a day of calm after the match-day crowds. Return ferry is around $15/person.

Fraser Valley & Wine Country

Drive east through the Fraser Valley to reach a different BC entirely — agricultural land, working farms, and some excellent wine country. The Fraser Valley wine region (around Langley and Abbotsford, 45–60 minutes from Vancouver) has several good wineries doing tastings, including Township 7 and Chaberton Estate.

The Fort Langley National Historic Site (50km east of Vancouver) is the birthplace of British Columbia — a restored fur trade fort with costumed interpreters and fascinating BC history. Good for families, and the Langley village nearby has excellent bakeries and cafés.

Tips for Day-Tripping During the World Cup

Plan around match days: If you have a ticket to a Vancouver match, keep day trips to non-match days. The day after a match (especially an evening match) is ideal — you have a full free day.

Book transport in advance: BC Ferries, Whistler buses, and Sea-to-Sky Gondola tickets should all be booked ahead for July travel. Don't assume walk-up availability.

Check the weather: Vancouver's weather is variable in July. Whistler can be rainy when Vancouver is clear and vice versa. Check the mountain forecast the night before and adjust plans accordingly.

Get up early: For any trip involving the Sea-to-Sky Highway, leave Vancouver by 7:30am to beat weekend traffic. It's worth it.
